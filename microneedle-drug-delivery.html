Microneedle-based Drug Delivery

Microneedles are minimally invasive delivery platforms designed to penetrate the outer skin barrier and deliver therapeutic agents into local tissue.

Why Microneedles?

Compared with conventional hypodermic needles, microneedles can reduce pain, improve patient compliance, and enable localized drug delivery. This makes them useful for applications such as vaccines, transdermal delivery, and local cancer therapy.

Types of Microneedles

  • Solid microneedles
  • Coated microneedles
  • Dissolving microneedles
  • Hydrogel-forming microneedles
  • Hollow microneedles

Materials

Microneedles can be fabricated from various materials, including metals, silicon, sugars, biodegradable polymers, and hydrogels.

My Research Interest

I am particularly interested in GelMA- and PLGA-based microneedle systems for localized and sustained delivery of therapeutic agents to superficial tumors such as melanoma.
